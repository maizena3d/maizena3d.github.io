Use a corn-starch-based material for 3D printing

  • Corn starch is a cost-effective, biodegradable material that can be used as an alternative to ABS plastic for 3D printer feed input.
  • A box of corn starch at the store is about $1.50, or about $500 per metric ton.
  • It is easy to clean up.
  • It is biodegradable.
  • Output can be flexible and expandible.
  • In the right regime, the printed material expands when cooled.
  • It is easy to add color.

Simple materials

Applications

  • Prototypes at scale
  • Print flexible molds for prototyping.
  • Print your own corn starch snacks!
  • Print packing material for complex structures.
  • Making molds (medical applications), like prosthetics.
  • Packing material for pharmaceutical shipping.
  • Hobbyist toys and models.
  • Create a filament polymer to retro-fit existing 3D printers.
